# Portfolio

A collection of quantitative economics and data science projects spanning 
financial econometrics, structural modelling, machine learning and applied 
econometrics.

## Projects

### [ECB Monetary Policy and Eurozone Financial Markets](./ecb-monetary-policy-vecm)
A trivariate VECM analysis examining how ECB unconventional monetary policy 
— including negative interest rates and quantitative easing — affected 
Eurozone equity markets and exchange rates over 2015–2018. Implements unit 
root testing, Johansen cointegration, impulse response functions and a 
Robinson (1995) long memory test. Built in R and Stata.

### [Structural Entry Models — MLE and Simulation](./structural-entry-models-mle)
Maximum likelihood estimation of the Bresnahan and Reiss (1991) binary 
entry model, implemented from scratch in MATLAB with analytical gradient 
and Hessian, cross-validated against Stata. Extends to a Berry (1992) 
simulation framework incorporating firm heterogeneity. Scored 100%.

### [What Drives International Sporting Success?](./sporting-success-economic-determinants)
An investigation into the relationship between economic strength and 
international sporting success, spanning Olympic medal counts and FIFA 
World Rankings across 2000–2024. Uses OLS and Poisson regression to 
identify key predictors and predict Paris 2024 medal counts, achieving 
a mean absolute error of 3.63 — an improvement on Bernard and Busse 
(2004). Built in Python.

### [Brexit and the London Stock Exchange](./brexit-lse-dissertation)
Undergraduate dissertation investigating firm-level heterogeneity in stock 
market responses to the Brexit referendum. Examines why some firms were 
affected more than others using econometric methods in Stata.

## Technical Skills

- **Methods:** Time series analysis, maximum likelihood estimation,
