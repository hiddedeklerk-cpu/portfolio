# Brexit and the London Stock Exchange

## Overview
This undergraduate dissertation investigates firm-level heterogeneity in 
stock market responses to the Brexit referendum. Rather than examining the 
average market response, the project asks why some firms listed on the 
London Stock Exchange performed significantly worse than others following 
the referendum result, and what firm and industry characteristics explain 
this heterogeneity.

This was submitted as an undergraduate dissertation for the BSc Economics 
and Econometrics degree at the University of Nottingham, achieving a mark 
of 73% (First Class).

## Key Questions
- Did the Brexit referendum have a heterogeneous impact across firms listed
on the London Stock Exchange?
- What firm-level and industry-level characteristics explain variation in 
stock market responses?
- What does this tell us about how financial markets price political and 
regulatory uncertainty?

## Methodology
- Event study analysis centred on the Brexit referendum result
- Cross-sectional regression analysis of firm-level abnormal returns
- Investigation of firm characteristics including export exposure, sector,
size and ownership structure
- Implemented in STATA
